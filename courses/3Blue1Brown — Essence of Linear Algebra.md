# 3Blue1Brown — Essence of Linear Algebra

**~3 hours of visual linear algebra. Watch before Strang for geometric intuition.**

## What It Covers

Vectors, linear transformations, matrix multiplication, determinants, eigenvalues/eigenvectors, and change of basis — all explained through animations. The key insight: matrices are transformations of space, not just grids of numbers.

## Why Watch This First

After this series, you'll see matrix multiplication as composing transformations, determinants as scaling factors, and eigenvectors as the axes that don't change direction. This geometric intuition makes Strang's MIT 18.06 course much easier to absorb.

## Key Takeaways

- Matrices are linear transformations of space — not just grids of numbers to multiply
- The determinant measures how a transformation scales area (or volume) — zero determinant means information is lost
- Eigenvectors are the directions that don't rotate under a transformation — eigenvalues are how much they stretch
- Change of basis is the key to understanding why diagonalization, PCA, and similarity transforms work
