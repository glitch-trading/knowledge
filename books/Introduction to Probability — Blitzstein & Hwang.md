# Introduction to Probability — Blitzstein & Hwang

**The companion text to [[Harvard Stat 110]]. Free PDF available.**

## Why Read This

This is one of the best introductions to probability ever written. It builds intuition through storytelling and examples rather than dry formalism. The writing is unusually clear for a math textbook, and the exercises are excellent — many are drawn from real applications.

It pairs perfectly with the Harvard Stat 110 lecture videos (also free). Together, they provide a rock-solid probability foundation that everything else in quantitative finance builds on.

## Key Takeaways

- **Conditioning is everything.** The book drives home that conditional probability and conditioning are the most powerful tools in probability. Bayes' theorem, LOTE (law of total expectation), and conditional expectation are the core techniques.
- **Named distributions matter.** Deep understanding of Bernoulli, Binomial, Geometric, Poisson, Exponential, Normal, Gamma, Beta distributions — their properties, relationships, and when each arises naturally.
- **Story proofs.** Many identities are proven by constructing a "story" — a physical scenario where both sides of the equation count the same thing differently. Builds deep intuition.
- **Markov chains.** The final chapters cover Markov chains, which are directly relevant to modeling market microstructure and trading dynamics.
- **Common pitfalls.** Gambler's fallacy, prosecutor's fallacy, Simpson's paradox, base rate neglect — the book systematically addresses the traps that trip up practitioners.
