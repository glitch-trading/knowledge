# The Man Who Solved the Market — Gregory Zuckerman

**The story of Jim Simons and Renaissance Technologies — the most successful quant fund in history.**

## Why Read This

Renaissance's Medallion Fund returned ~66% annually before fees for over 30 years. This book is the most detailed account of how they built it — the culture, the people, the approach. It's not a trading manual, but it reveals the mindset: hire scientists (not finance people), let the data speak, size for survival, and constantly search for new signals.

Key lesson: Renaissance succeeded by treating markets as a data problem, not a finance problem. They hired mathematicians, physicists, and computer scientists who brought fresh perspectives uncorrupted by financial orthodoxy.

## Key Takeaways

- **Signals are everywhere, edge is rare.** Renaissance found thousands of small, fleeting patterns. No single signal was remarkable — the ensemble was.
- **Infrastructure is edge.** Their data pipeline and execution infrastructure were years ahead of competitors. Speed of research iteration mattered as much as the research itself.
- **Culture of secrecy and collaboration.** Internal openness (everyone shares signals) combined with external secrecy (no one talks outside). Non-competes enforced ruthlessly.
- **Edges decay.** Even Renaissance constantly retires old signals and searches for new ones. Complacency is death.
