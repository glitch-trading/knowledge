# Interpreting Prediction Market Prices — Manski

**Authors:** Charles F. Manski
**Year:** 2006

## Summary

Challenges the common assumption that prediction market prices equal event probabilities. Shows that the mapping from prices to probabilities depends on trader risk preferences, wealth distribution, and belief heterogeneity. A necessary corrective before treating prediction market prices as ground truth.

## Key Results

- Prediction market prices equal mean beliefs only under restrictive conditions (risk-neutral traders with identical wealth)
- With risk-averse traders, prices can systematically deviate from true consensus probabilities
- Prices in the 0.10-0.90 range are reasonably well-calibrated; extreme prices (near 0 or 1) are less reliable
- Thin markets with few participants magnify the distortion — liquidity matters for price quality
