# Core Principles

Keep these in mind throughout the entire journey.

## 1. Pen and paper
Derive equations yourself. Every time a paper says "a simple computation yields," stop and do it before reading on.

## 2. Build alongside learning
Every concept should have a corresponding piece of code within days of learning it. Theory without implementation is entertainment, not education.

## 3. Observe before trading
Spend at least 2 weeks watching live markets and logging data before placing any real trade.

## 4. Size for survival
Your first 6 months of live trading should use amounts you'd be comfortable lighting on fire. The tuition is the experience, not the P&L.

## 5. The edge is not the code
Vibe coding the execution layer is fine. But the signal — *why* this price is wrong and *why* nobody else has fixed it yet — requires genuine understanding.

## 6. Edges decay
Every profitable strategy has a shelf life. Build the habit of constantly searching for new edges while harvesting existing ones.

## 7. Risk management is the strategy
The best signal in the world paired with bad [[Position Sizing]] and no kill switch will ruin you. The worst-case scenario isn't losing money — it's losing money you can't afford to lose.

## 8. Be honest about what you don't know
Markets punish overconfidence faster than anything else. If a strategy is working and you don't understand why, reduce size until you do.

## 9. Estimation error is the real enemy
Full Kelly, unconstrained Markowitz, ML with too many features — all fail for the same reason: overfitting noisy data. The gap between theory and practice is always estimation error.

## 10. Tools are democratized, conviction isn't
Anyone can access QuantLib, Polygon.io, PyTorch. Edge lives in unique data, unique models, or unique execution — not better pip installs. Borrowed edge expires.

## 11. Math is the moat
The ability to derive *why* Itô's lemma has an extra term, why discounted prices are martingales — mathematical fluency separates quants who build edge from those who borrow it.
