**Authors:** John L. Kelly Jr.
**Year:** 1956

## Summary

The original paper deriving the Kelly criterion from information theory. Shows that maximizing the expected logarithm of wealth (geometric growth rate) yields the optimal fraction of capital to wager. Short, readable, and surprisingly accessible.

## Key Results

- Optimal bet fraction: `f* = p - q` for even-money bets (where p = win probability, q = 1-p)
- General formula: `f* = (bp - q) / b` where b = odds received
- Maximizes long-run geometric growth rate — any other strategy grows slower with probability 1
- Over-betting (above Kelly) increases variance without improving growth; extreme over-betting leads to ruin
- In practice, use fractional Kelly (½ or ¼) because real-world edge and variance estimates are uncertain
