**Authors:** Jack L. Treynor
**Year:** 1987

## Summary

Intuitive treatment of why market makers exist, how they set spreads, and what risks they bear. Decomposes the dealer's problem into inventory management and adverse selection — the same two forces modeled formally in [[Avellaneda-Stoikov]].

## Key Results

- Dealers provide immediacy — they let traders transact now instead of waiting for a counterparty
- The bid-ask spread compensates for two distinct risks: inventory carrying cost and trading against informed counterparties
- Wider spreads emerge when adverse selection risk is high (more informed traders) or inventory risk is high (volatile assets)
