**A rigorous but accessible statistics reference covering probability through inference.**

## Why Read This

Most statistics textbooks are either too applied (cookbook recipes) or too theoretical (measure theory). Wasserman hits the sweet spot — rigorous enough to build real understanding, concise enough to actually finish. Chapters 1-13 cover everything from probability axioms through hypothesis testing, MLE, and regression in a way that makes the math clear without drowning in proofs.

For quant finance, this is the stats foundation that lets you properly evaluate whether a signal is real or noise. The treatment of hypothesis testing, multiple comparisons, and maximum likelihood estimation is directly applicable.

## Key Takeaways

- **MLE is the workhorse.** Maximum likelihood estimation is how you fit distributions to data properly — understand likelihood functions, log-likelihood, and Fisher information.
- **Hypothesis testing as a BS detector.** p-values, confidence intervals, and their limitations. The multiple comparisons problem is critical for strategy evaluation.
- **Nonparametric methods exist.** Don't assume normality when you don't have to — bootstrap, permutation tests, and kernel density estimation.
- **Bayesian vs frequentist.** Covers both perspectives, which matters for combining prior beliefs with market data.
