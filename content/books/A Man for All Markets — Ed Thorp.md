**Kelly criterion intuition and the quantitative mindset. The original quant.**

## Key Takeaways

- **Kelly Criterion is optimal but dangerous at full size.** Mathematically, Kelly maximizes long-run growth rate. In practice, use fractional Kelly (½ or ¼) because your edge estimate is noisy, tails are fatter than you think, and full Kelly drawdowns are stomach-churning.
- **Find structural edges, not informational ones.** Thorp's edges came from mathematical mispricing (warrants, convertible bonds, blackjack), not from knowing something others didn't. Structural edges are more durable than informational ones.
- **Size for survival.** The worst-case scenario isn't a bad trade — it's a bad trade that wipes you out. Thorp's entire approach is built around never risking ruin, even when the expected value is positive.
- **Quantitative thinking applied everywhere.** Thorp approached blackjack, warrants, and markets with the same framework: model the system, find the edge, size the bet, manage the risk. The domain changes; the method doesn't.
- **Markets are not efficient, but exploiting inefficiencies requires discipline.** Mispricings exist and persist, but capturing them demands rigorous position sizing, hedging, and emotional control.
