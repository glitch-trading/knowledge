## What It Is

For a strictly convex, differentiable function $f$ on a convex domain, the Bregman divergence generated by $f$ measures the gap between $f$ and its linear approximation at a reference point:

$$D_f(\mathbf{p} \,\|\, \mathbf{q}) = f(\mathbf{p}) - f(\mathbf{q}) - \langle \nabla f(\mathbf{q}),\, \mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q} \rangle$$

It is non-negative, zero iff $\mathbf{p} = \mathbf{q}$, **asymmetric** in general, and **not a metric** (no triangle inequality). The choice of $f$ specializes $D_f$ to many familiar objects:

| Generator $f$ | Domain | Bregman divergence $D_f$ |
| --- | --- | --- |
| $\tfrac{1}{2}\lVert \mathbf{x} \rVert_2^2$ | $\mathbb{R}^n$ | Squared Euclidean distance $\tfrac{1}{2}\lVert \mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q} \rVert_2^2$ |
| $\sum_i x_i \log x_i - x_i$ | $\mathbb{R}_{>0}^n$ | Generalized KL (extended-KL) |
| $\sum_i x_i \log x_i$ on simplex | $\Delta^{n-1}$ | [[KL Divergence]] |
| $-\sum_i \log x_i$ | $\mathbb{R}_{>0}^n$ | Itakura–Saito divergence |
| $\log\!\bigl(\sum_i e^{x_i / b}\bigr) \cdot b$ | $\mathbb{R}^n$ | LMSR-cost divergence (reduces to KL on the simplex) |

The last row is the [[Logarithmic Market Scoring Rule]] cost function — i.e., the natural divergence on an LMSR-priced [[Prediction Markets|prediction market]] *is* KL.

## Why It Matters

**One framework, many algorithms.** Bregman divergences unify projection, optimization, and online-learning algorithms that would otherwise look unrelated:

- **Bregman projection.** $\arg\min_{\mathbf{p} \in C} D_f(\mathbf{p} \,\|\, \mathbf{q})$ for a convex set $C$ generalizes Euclidean projection. Under $f = \tfrac{1}{2}\lVert \cdot \rVert_2^2$ this is least-squares projection; under negative-entropy $f$ it is the [[Maximum Entropy Principle|max-ent / I-projection]] used throughout information geometry.
- **Mirror descent / online convex optimization.** The standard subgradient method replaces Euclidean steps with steps measured in $D_f$. The "mirror map" $\nabla f$ converts between primal and dual representations; this is how multiplicative-weights, exponentiated gradient, and natural-gradient methods derive from a single template.
- **Proper scoring rules.** Every proper scoring rule for probabilistic forecasts corresponds to a Bregman divergence between the forecast and the realized outcome. Log loss ↔ KL; Brier score ↔ squared Euclidean.
- **Pythagorean identity.** Under a Bregman projection $\mathbf{p}^* = \arg\min_{\mathbf{p} \in C} D_f(\mathbf{p} \,\|\, \mathbf{q})$, for any $\mathbf{r} \in C$:

$$D_f(\mathbf{r} \,\|\, \mathbf{q}) \ge D_f(\mathbf{r} \,\|\, \mathbf{p}^*) + D_f(\mathbf{p}^* \,\|\, \mathbf{q})$$

A clean generalization of the Pythagorean theorem; underpins convergence proofs.

**Why it matters specifically for prediction markets.**

- LMSR's cost function $C(\mathbf{q}) = b\log\sum_i e^{q_i/b}$ is a convex potential whose associated Bregman divergence is KL on the simplex.
- The arbitrage-free price projection in a [[Combinatorial Prediction Markets|combinatorial prediction market]] is the Bregman projection of the current market price onto the marginal polytope.
- The **maximum extractable profit** of an arbitrage is exactly the Bregman divergence between the projected (arbitrage-free) state and the current market state. Information distance literally equals money.
- The same identity is why moving a market price from $0.05$ to $0.15$ is a larger "informational" event than moving it from $0.45$ to $0.55$ — the Bregman / KL geometry is steeper near the boundary.

**Connection to other notes.**

- $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p \,\|\, q)$ as expected log-growth of a Kelly bettor (see [[KL Divergence]]) is the simplest illustration of the "Bregman divergence = profit" identity.
- Max-ent solutions ([[Maximum Entropy Principle]]) are Bregman projections onto a constraint set from the uniform prior.
- Mirror descent generalizes most online-learning algorithms used in adaptive market-making.

## Key Equations

**General definition:**
$$D_f(\mathbf{p} \,\|\, \mathbf{q}) = f(\mathbf{p}) - f(\mathbf{q}) - \langle \nabla f(\mathbf{q}),\, \mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q} \rangle$$

**KL as a Bregman divergence** (with $f(\mathbf{p}) = \sum_i p_i \log p_i$ on the simplex):
$$D_f(\mathbf{p} \,\|\, \mathbf{q}) = \sum_i p_i \log \frac{p_i}{q_i} = D_{\mathrm{KL}}(\mathbf{p} \,\|\, \mathbf{q})$$

**Bregman projection onto a convex set $C$:**
$$\mathbf{p}^* = \arg\min_{\mathbf{p} \in C} D_f(\mathbf{p} \,\|\, \mathbf{q})$$

**Pythagorean inequality:**
$$D_f(\mathbf{r} \,\|\, \mathbf{q}) \ge D_f(\mathbf{r} \,\|\, \mathbf{p}^*) + D_f(\mathbf{p}^* \,\|\, \mathbf{q}), \quad \forall \mathbf{r} \in C$$
