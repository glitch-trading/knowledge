**~3 hours of visual, intuition-first calculus. Watch before drilling exercises.**

## What It Covers

Derivatives, integrals, chain rule, and Taylor series — explained through animations that make the concepts click visually. Not a replacement for practice problems, but the best starting point for building intuition about what calculus *means* geometrically.

## Why Watch This First

Grant Sanderson's visual approach makes "rate of change" and "area under the curve" genuinely intuitive before you encounter the formalism. After watching, derivatives and integrals feel natural rather than mechanical. Then use Khan Academy for drill.

## Key Takeaways

- Derivatives measure instantaneous rate of change — the ratio `df/dx` as `dx` shrinks to zero
- Integration is the inverse of differentiation, but geometrically it's accumulating infinitesimal slices of area
- The chain rule is composition of rates — if `y` changes 3x per unit of `x`, and `z` changes 2x per unit of `y`, then `z` changes 6x per unit of `x`
- Taylor series approximate any smooth function locally as a polynomial — the basis for all linearization in finance
