**Joe Blitzstein. Excellent lecturer, free on YouTube.**

## What It Covers
Core probability: random variables, distributions, expected value, variance, conditional probability, independence, Bayes' theorem. Also covers key distributions (normal, exponential, Poisson) in depth.

## Key Takeaways

- Conditioning is the soul of statistics — most problems become tractable once you condition on the right thing
- Named distributions aren't arbitrary — each models a specific real-world generating process (Poisson = count of rare events, exponential = waiting time, etc.)
- Story proofs are more powerful than algebraic proofs — understanding *why* a result is true beats memorizing the derivation
- Expected value is linear regardless of dependence — one of the most useful facts in probability
