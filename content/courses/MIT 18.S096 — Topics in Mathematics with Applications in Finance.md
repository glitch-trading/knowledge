**MIT's quant finance math course. Lectures 17-21 supplement Shreve for stochastic calculus.**

## What It Covers

Covers the mathematical tools used in quantitative finance: probability, stochastic processes, Itô calculus, Black-Scholes, portfolio theory, time series, and regression. Lectures are given by both MIT faculty and industry practitioners.

## Key Lectures

- **Lectures 17-21:** Stochastic calculus, Itô's lemma, SDEs, Black-Scholes derivation — use these to supplement [[Stochastic Calculus for Finance II — Steven Shreve]]
- **Lectures on portfolio theory and CAPM** — mean-variance optimization in practice
- **Guest lectures from practitioners** — bridge between theory and industry application

## Key Takeaways

- Itô's lemma is the chain rule for stochastic calculus — the extra `½σ²f''` term comes from `(dW)² = dt`
- Black-Scholes is just Itô's lemma applied to a delta-hedged portfolio, then setting drift to risk-free rate
- Practitioner lectures show that real quant work is 80% data cleaning and infrastructure, 20% math
- CAPM and mean-variance optimization break down when returns aren't normal — but the framework is still the starting point
