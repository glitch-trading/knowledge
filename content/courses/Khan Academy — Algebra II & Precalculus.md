**Free. Skip what you know, but don't skip logarithms and exponentials.**

## What It Covers

Logarithms (natural log especially), exponentials, summation/product notation, multi-variable function notation. You'll see `e^(-γx)`, `ln(1 + γ/k)`, and nested functions everywhere in quant finance — these need to be effortless to read.

## Key Takeaways

- Natural log `ln` and `e` are everywhere in finance — compound returns, Black-Scholes, decay rates, information theory
- Exponential growth/decay governs interest rates, option time decay (theta), and mean-reverting processes
- Summation and product notation (`Σ`, `Π`) must be fluent — portfolio returns, likelihood functions, and series expansions all use them
- Multi-variable function notation is prerequisite for partial derivatives and stochastic calculus
